Abstract: Machine learning models have become increasingly large, posing significant challenges for deployment on edge devices. Prior work has explored quantization and pruning, but these often lead to unacceptable accuracy degradation. In this paper, we propose Quantum-Annealed Distillation (QAD), a novel knowledge distillation framework that leverages simulated quantum annealing to optimize the student model's weight initialization before traditional gradient descent begins. By identifying global minima in the student's initial loss landscape, QAD allows a 10M-parameter student to match the accuracy of a 500M-parameter teacher on the ImageNet benchmark. Furthermore, we introduce a hardware-aware loss term that penalizes memory-intensive operations, ensuring the resulting model runs efficiently on ARM Cortex-M microcontrollers. Experimental results demonstrate a 3x speedup in inference time with negligible loss in predictive performance.
